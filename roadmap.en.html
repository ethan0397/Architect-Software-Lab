Overview

A software architect is not just someone who draws diagrams. The role requires understanding business goals, translating them into a working system, and keeping that system adaptable through many rounds of change.

Phase Focus Expected outcome
Months 1-2 Code fundamentals and patterns Write clear, testable code and recognize design problems early
Months 3-4 Application architecture and distributed systems Design better boundaries and understand consistency, retry, and timeout issues
Months 5-6 Trade-offs, system design, and architect mindset Choose the right structure and justify decisions through ADRs

Six-month plan

Month 1

Technical foundations

OOP, SOLID, cohesion, coupling, clean code, refactoring, unit testing, and testability.

Month 2

Core design patterns

Strategy, Factory Method, Builder, Adapter, Facade, Observer, Command, Decorator, Repository, and Dependency Injection.

Month 3

Application Architecture

Layered architecture, clean architecture, hexagonal architecture, modular monolith, and the start of the Hard Parts track with coupling, modularity, and decomposition.

Month 4

Data and Distributed Systems

Transactions, locking, cache-aside, message queues, eventual consistency, idempotency, timeout, retry, circuit breaker, plus data decomposition and service granularity.

Month 5

Architecture Styles and Trade-offs

Monolith, modular monolith, microservices, event-driven architecture, serverless, DDD strategic design, along with data ownership, distributed access, workflow, and sagas.

Month 6

System Design and Architect Mindset

Non-functional requirements, capacity estimation, service boundaries, observability, security basics, architecture review, contracts, data mesh, and trade-off analysis.

Book Track: Software Architecture: The Hard Parts

How it fits into the roadmap

  • Month 3: coupling, modularity, decomposition
  • Month 4: data decomposition, service granularity
  • Month 5: reuse, data ownership, distributed access, workflow, sagas
  • Month 6: contracts, analytical data, data mesh, trade-off analysis
Why add this track This is the part that shifts the roadmap from “knowing concepts” to “making decisions.” If architecture foundations are the skeleton, this book is where you train on the hardest distributed architecture problems.

Book Track: Fundamentals of Software Architecture

How it fits into the roadmap

  • Months 1-2: architect role, modularity, characteristics
  • Month 3: governance, quanta, component thinking
  • Months 4-5: architecture styles survey and style selection
  • Month 6: decisions, risk, diagramming, leadership
What this track does If `Hard Parts` gives you distributed architecture depth, `Fundamentals` gives you the base language and evaluation model to understand architecture as an engineering discipline.

Weekly rhythm

Suggested cadence

  • 2 hours of theory
  • 3 hours of small coding exercises
  • 2 hours of refactoring old code
  • 1 hour of diagramming
  • 1-2 hours of review and note-taking
Remember If you only read and never draw or refactor, the knowledge will not stick. The effective loop is: read -> code -> refactor -> draw -> review.

Self-check milestones

By the end of six months, you should be able to

  • Distinguish between design patterns and architecture styles
  • Explain trade-offs clearly
  • Design good boundaries and dependency direction
  • Explain why data consistency is difficult
  • Present a coherent system design to a team
